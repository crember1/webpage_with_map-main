Introduction

Bike sharing, an up-and-coming transportation style in various cities and urban areas, gives passengers easy access to move around town. In Charlotte, North Carolina, B-Cycle - a docked Bike Sharing MoD system - provides 343 bikes and 34 stations throughout the Queen City for easy transportation. Charlotte B-cycle launched in 2012 with only 200 bikes and 20 stations, but through various sponsorships, grants, and support over the last eight years, the company has grown to its current size. In addition to providing its services, Charlotte B-cycle also works to support the local community. When you see one of their bikes whizzing around town, you'll notice each bike sports a piece of art created by a local artist.

Conclusion

Our maps express a story. In figure three, most of the bike stations are located inside interstate 77 and 485 in the inner city accumulating the most usage combined while outside the city has less stations. We believe people in the inner city use these bikes to commute to and from work. The stations south of Charlotte are in South end, a high commercial area, which mean people commute to and from for entertainment. In addition, in figure five, the number of bus stations compared to bike stations are exponentially larger showing a greater interest in buses versus bikes. As you can see in the figure six, there is not that many bike lanes in the city; therefore, most bikes will either be on the sidewalk or a part of traffic creating potiential self risk.

Discussion

Based off all given data, these are some suggestions we have to improve the relationships between stations and other attributes in Charlotte.

1. Expand B-Cycle station locations to more residential areas outside of the city to give commuters more mobility options.
2. Coordinate future expansion CATS bus stop locations to all B-Cycle station locations, giving those using the CATS bus system more mobility options going to and from the bus stops.
3. Expand bike lane infrastructure within the city to reach more B-Cycle station locations.
